Five named frameworks. Each entry: what it is, why it works, TBP applicability, specific test to run.

## Framework 1: MAGIC — social to email

Acronym for the five ways to grow email list from social/YouTube.

- **M — Magnets**: lead magnets. Solve one narrow problem, under 15 min to consume. Never build from scratch. Repurpose past newsletters, threads, video scripts into "lazy lead magnets." One great past post = one new lead magnet.
- **A — Asks**: pre-CTA and post-CTA on social.
  - Pre-CTA: 24h before sending. Structure: what you wrote + 3 bullets + 1 CTA + where to go. Creates urgency.
  - Post-CTA: after sending. Structure: big result / outcome + address objections + CTA. Creates FOMO.
- **G — Giveaways**: comment-to-get giveaways on LinkedIn, X, IG. User comments a keyword, you DM a link to an email-gated landing page. Algorithm boost from comments drives more reach. On LinkedIn, the DM must end with "can you let me know if the link works?" to trigger ~30% reply rate and dodge spam detection.
- **I — Inbox DM**: warm DM to every new follower within 24h, asking them to subscribe. Manual at small scale. ManyChat on IG. VA when volume crosses 50+ new followers/week.
- **C — Complete profile**: social bio optimized for one CTA. **Kill any linktree**. Linktree converts under 10% to email. Single-link to one newsletter landing page converts 50%+.

### TBP applicability: HIGH, now.

Easiest wins first: C (bio cleanup, zero dependency), then A (pre/post-CTAs), then M (lazy lead magnets), then G (comment-to-get), then I (warm DMs).

## Framework 2: Post-signup survey — first-party data + phone capture

Right after signup, serve a 5-7 question multiple-choice survey. Last question asks for phone number opt-in for SMS (use live events as the reason).

McGarry's results at scale:
- 87% of new subscribers complete the survey
- 17% share phone number without being pushed
- He's made $100K+ from SMS marketing of webinars/events without "really trying"

Why this matters:
- **Segmentation data** for targeted sends and automations
- **Phone numbers** for SMS (98% open rate vs. 40-50% for email)
- **Audience data** to pitch sponsors later (advertisers buy WHO, not how many)
- Works on beehiiv natively. Typeform or HighLevel also work.

### TBP applicability: HIGH, now.

Set it up before we're big enough to need it. Compounds from day one.

## Framework 3: WEAC — welcome email for deliverability

Five-part structure for the first email a new subscriber gets:

- **W — Welcome**: 1-2 sentences, thank them for joining.
- **E — Expectations**: what the newsletter covers, when it sends, why it's worth reading. 1-3 sentences. "Sell them on opening future emails."
- **A — Primary inbox**: ask them to move the email out of Promotions tab. Add a one-liner on HOW (Gmail: drag to Primary, Apple Mail: mark as VIP).
- **A — Ask for reply**: ask for a **ONE-WORD reply** (e.g., "ok", "yes"). Never open-ended. Open-ended kills reply rate because people have to think.
- **C — Click a link**: at least one link, ideally the promised lead magnet. The click action improves deliverability reputation.

Why: three actions (click, move-to-primary, reply) tell inbox providers your emails are wanted. Your FUTURE emails land in Primary for more subscribers, not just this one. Inbox reputation is a list-wide asset.

**Critical rule**: DO NOT use double opt-in unless legally required (EU). Loses 30%+ of subs who never confirm.

### TBP applicability: HIGH, now.

See `backlog.md` item C-003 (welcome email v2) and C-004 (email #2 for the open-ended PMF question).

### Targets

- Welcome email open rate: 60-80%
- Welcome email click rate: 10-20%
- Reply rate: track as list-health proxy

## Framework 4: Deliverability detox — Base Sending Segment (BSS)

Problem: sending to subscribers who haven't opened in months drags down inbox reputation for everyone. Solution: four-week sending progression that starts narrow and widens.

Create a dynamic "base sending segment" — subs who are either new or recently engaged.

- **Week 1, BSS1**: signed up last 10 days OR 1+ open last 30 OR 1+ click last 60
- **Week 2, BSS2**: signed up last 7 days OR 1+ open last 45 OR 1+ click last 90
- **Week 3, BSS3**: signed up last 21 days OR 1+ open last 60 OR 1+ click last 120
- **Week 4, BSS4**: signed up last 30 days OR 1+ open last 90 OR 1+ click last 180

McGarry's result: sending to smaller engaged segment (56K → 40K) got MORE unique opens and higher open rate (40% → 53%) because inbox placement improved.

After the 4-week detox, keep sending to BSS4 by default. Send to full list only when it makes strategic sense.

### TBP applicability: LATER. Park until month 3+.

We don't have enough send history or list size for this to matter yet. Revisit when we cross ~2K subs and have 60+ days of send data.

## Framework 5: Newsletter flywheel — ADAPT

Every newsletter edition becomes 5+ repurposed assets:

- **A — Amplify**: take one paragraph or idea, expand it into a full standalone social post.
- **D — Direct**: summarize the whole edition as one long LinkedIn post or X thread.
- **A — Ask**: pull a question or debate from the content, post as engagement bait (poll, open question).
- **P — Pull**: excerpt one standout quote or sentence as a graphic or short post.
- **T — Transcribe**: turn the newsletter into a video script, slide deck, or podcast segment.

Plus the orbiting actions:
- Auto-mirror the newsletter as a blog post (SEO + AI search surface)
- Pre-CTA 24h before send
- Post-CTA 24h after send
- Optional: comment-to-get giveaway mid-week using the edition as a lead magnet

### Weekly rhythm (McGarry's example, adapted for TBP Saturday cadence)

- **Friday**: Pre-CTA teasing Saturday
- **Saturday**: Newsletter ships + blog post mirrors
- **Sunday**: Post-CTA recapping, FOMO framing
- **Monday-Friday**: One ADAPT post per day (Amplify, Direct, Ask, Pull, Transcribe)
- **Mid-week** (every 2-3 weeks): comment-to-get giveaway using the edition as magnet

### Math

One newsletter = 7+ pieces of social content per week = 350+ per year from one weekly source.
